Queues

A queue is an ordered collection of items where addition and removal of data items takes place at different ends. The end where data items are added is called the rear and the end where items are removed is called the front.The most recently added item to the queue is at the rear and the item that has been longest in the queue is at the front. The ordering principle of a queue is FIFO(first in first out).

An analogy that can be used to explain queues is a waiting line in the bank. Assuming everyone who comes into the bank wants to see the bank tellers, they make a line and the first person on the line will be attended to before the next person and the last person in the line will be worked upon last.There is no skipping and a person cannot be worked upon until he has waited enough to be at the front of the line.
Another anology would be keystrokes of a computer keyboard.Whenever an individual is typing, the characters that are pressed first appear on the screen before the chracters that are pressed last
The queue operations include;

  • Enqueue: Adds a new item to the rear of the queue
  • Dequeue: Removes the front item from the queue
  • Peek: Returns the item at the front of the queue
  • Size: Returns the number of items in the queue.Takes no parameters and returns an int
